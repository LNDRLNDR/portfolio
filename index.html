What is a Dynamic Overlay for Rocket League?

The esports scene of Rocket League has come a long way. With millions of players and a thriving competitive scene, it's become one of the most popular games on the market. But with so many people streaming and watching Rocket League matches, it can be hard to stand out from the crowd.

That's where the Broadcasting Overlay HUD for Rocket League comes in. By adding a dynamic overlay to your stream, you can create a more professional and engaging viewing experience for your audience. The overlay will display key information about the match, including the score, timer, and team names, so viewers always know what's happening. And because the overlay is fully automated, it frees up the casters to focus on the gameplay, rather than worrying about the technical details of the stream.

RSC EU

This project made for RSC EU contains a Rocket League Game HUD, an overlay screen for when a goal is scored with customizable sponsor slots. The win screen shows who won the current game and also gives the viewer information on the series score. Up next is the post game statistics screen, this contains all sorts of stats from points, goals assets, but also boost consumption and field domination. When a series is done we can compare each team with the post series stats screen, this shows the performance of each team in the series. At the end of the series the MVP screen can be shown to highlight the most valuable player in th series. The caster screen can dynamically be adjusted to fit the amount of casters required from one caster up to three casters. This caster screen can also show predictions for the upcoming series as well as customizable text to give the viewer more information. And finally an intermission screen which can be used as a "Starting soon" screen, "Be right back" screen, or any intermission you might need since the text is fully customizable.

SaltMine3

Introducing Salt Mine 3 hosted by Johnnyboi, a meticulously crafted Rocket League overlay and in-game Heads-Up Display (HUD) experience designed with a focus on optimizing the 1vs1 gameplay. Elevating your Rocket League viewing experience, Salt Mine 3 brings several innovative features to the forefront.

The RL Game overlay introduces a respawn timer specifically tailored for demos, this gives the viewers even more insights into the game. The overlay goes beyond the ordinary by incorporating a dynamic win screen that varies based on the car used, adding a personalized touch to the victories.

Diving into the analytics, Salt Mine 3 features in-game pop-up displays showcasing advanced statistics, offering viewers a deeper insight into the match dynamics. To further enrich the viewer analysis, the overlay introduces a minimap functionality during replays, allowing you to really understand how a goal developped.

The overlay also provides a comprehensive series statistics screen to keep track of the performance over multiple matches. With its thoughtful design and feature-rich capabilities, Salt Mine 3 sets a new standard for Rocket League overlays.

Gen.G Mobil1 Racing

The next project is made for the organisation Gen.G which has a Rocket League team sponsored by Mobil1. The main game overlay contains a boost meter as well as a speed meter to give the viewer a perception of speed and give it a racing theme. The main scoreboard only contains the team logos with the full team names integrated into the team boost boxes to give it a clean aesthetic and also refer to formula 1. During the gameplay a customizable animated sponsor box is shown to display each sponsor to the viewer. While a goal replay happens a Minimap is shown to give the viewer a better understanding of how the goal developed.

Feer

This overlay is made for Feer (Twitch) and is on first sight a more simple overlay, with a lot of hidden features. First of all it has a series score overview box with player name, player nationality flags. In the game overlay the player names and nationality flags are also shown, it also shows the player or team boost meter at the very top so the viewer can keep track of the boost at all times.

G1

This was made for the "G1 Invitational" tournament with a prize pool of 10k and hosted by 'GAMERSFIRST' (aka G1). It's inspired by the NFL and uses a lot of squared shapes. During goal replays a Minimap (radar) is shown to give the viewer more insight into the play that caused the goal to happen. The series score is shown on the outsides of the score-bug (top header) and automatically adjusts from best of 0 up to best of 9. The post game stat screen shows stats like: score, goals, assists, saves, shots, demos and touches. This makes sure the viewer understands which players performed well and what their contribution was.

PSG (Paris Saint-Germain) x Tundra
